Comprehensive Stroke Center

Project Details

Role: Healthcare UX Designer
Duration: 8 months
Team: 3 designers 8 developers 5 clinicians
Tools: Sketch InVision Marvel
Year: 2023

Healthcare Challenge

Stroke care coordination required seamless communication between emergency responders, hospital staff, and specialists to ensure optimal patient outcomes. The existing systems were fragmented, leading to delays in critical care decisions and suboptimal patient treatment protocols. Time-sensitive interventions demanded a more efficient, integrated approach to care coordination and data management.

Clinical Research

We collaborated with neurologists, emergency physicians, and nursing staff to understand current workflows and pain points. Through shadowing sessions and stakeholder interviews, we identified critical moments in the stroke care pathway where digital tools could improve efficiency. Our research revealed the need for real-time communication tools and centralized patient data visualization.

System Design

The design process focused on creating intuitive interfaces that healthcare professionals could use under high-stress conditions. We developed role-based dashboards, streamlined data entry forms, and implemented clear visual hierarchies for critical patient information. The system architecture prioritized reliability, security, and compliance with healthcare regulations while maintaining ease of use.

Implementation

The comprehensive stroke center platform integrated with existing hospital systems while providing new tools for care coordination. Features included real-time patient tracking, automated alert systems, and collaborative care planning interfaces. The implementation process involved extensive training sessions and iterative refinements based on user feedback from healthcare professionals.

Clinical Impact

The new system reduced door-to-needle time by an average of 23 minutes and improved communication efficiency between care teams by 65%. Patient satisfaction scores increased significantly, and the hospital achieved Level 1 Stroke Center certification within one year of implementation. The platform now serves as a model for other healthcare facilities seeking to improve stroke care delivery.
